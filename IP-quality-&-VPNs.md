# IP quality & VPNs

### Can I provide using another VPN?
While it is technically possible, providing bandwidth through another VPN (e.g., Mullvad, Proton) is **strongly discouraged** and usually results in near-zero earnings.
* **IP Ranking:** URnetwork ranks IP addresses. IPs identified as VPN exit nodes are ranked at the bottom and rarely, if ever, win traffic contracts.
* **Multiplier Inefficiency:** A high country multiplier (e.g., 10x) multiplied by zero contracts still equals zero earnings.
* **Static IP Detection:** The network continuously scans for IP changes; switching to a VPN will be detected even if it has a static IP.

### IP Types & Reputation
* **Residential IPs:** Highly valued and most in demand.
* **Business/ISP IPs:** Generally perform well.
* **Datacenter IPs:** Often have lower demand but can still move traffic if they have very low latency and clean reputation.
* **Reputation Check:** Clean IPs with no history of abuse are prioritized. You can check your IP's status at tools like [ipfighter.com](https://ipfighter.com).

### Performance Factors
Beyond just the IP type, the network favors:
* **Low Latency:** Nodes with fast response times to the URnetwork API.
* **High Reliability:** Sustained 24/7 uptime.
* **Geo-Optimization:** Deploying IPs near their physical location.

> **Staff Advice:** Your residential IP—even if its "abuse score" isn't perfect—will almost always outperform a VPN or proxy exit in terms of real contract wins.
